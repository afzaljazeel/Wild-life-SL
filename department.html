Department of Wildlife Conservation (DWC)

The Department of Wildlife Conservation in Sri Lanka is typically tasked with the conservation and sustainable management of the country's rich biodiversity. Its primary objectives include the protection of wildlife habitats, the prevention of illegal activities such as poaching and wildlife trafficking, and the promotion of responsible ecotourism.

Key responsibilities of the Wildlife Department may include:


1. Biodiversity Conservation:

The department works towards the conservation of diverse ecosystems, including national parks, wildlife reserves, and sanctuaries. It aims to maintain a healthy and balanced ecosystem for various species of flora and fauna.


2. Law Enforcement:

The DWC enforces wildlife protection laws and regulations to combat illegal activities, such as hunting, fishing, and the trade of endangered species.


3. Research and Monitoring:

Conducting scientific research and monitoring of wildlife populations to understand their behavior, ecology, and health is often part of the department's mandate. This information helps in making informed decisions for conservation efforts.


4. Community Engagement:

Involving local communities in wildlife conservation is crucial. The department may work on initiatives to raise awareness, provide education, and involve communities in sustainable practices that contribute to biodiversity conservation.


5. Ecotourism Development:

Promoting responsible and sustainable ecotourism helps generate revenue for conservation efforts while providing economic opportunities for local communities.



Sri Lanka is known for its diverse and unique wildlife, including various species of elephants, leopards, birds, and marine life. The Department of Wildlife Conservation plays a pivotal role in ensuring the protection and preservation of this natural heritage for current and future generations. For the most up-to-date and accurate information, it's recommended to check with official government sources or the department's official website.
