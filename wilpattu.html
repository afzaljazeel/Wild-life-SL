WILPATTU NATIONAL PARK

Location and Size:

Yala National Park is situated in the southeastern part of Sri Lanka, in the Hambantota district. It is the second-largest national park in Sri Lanka, covering an area of approximately 978 square kilometers.

Biodiversity:

Yala is renowned for its rich biodiversity and is home to a variety of ecosystems, including dense jungles, scrublands, and coastal areas. The park is known for its high density of leopards, making it one of the best places in the world to spot these elusive big cats. Other wildlife includes elephants, sloth bears, deer, crocodiles, and a plethora of bird species.

Tourist Attractions and Activities:

Yala offers safari experiences that allow visitors to explore the diverse landscapes and observe the wildlife in their natural habitats. The park is divided into five blocks, with Block 1 being the most popular for safaris. Visitors can also explore historical sites like Sithulpawwa, an ancient Buddhist monastery within the park.
